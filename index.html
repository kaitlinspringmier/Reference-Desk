Total Transactions

Reference Librarians charted a total of 396 reference transactions. People are invited to call, instant message, or visit the reference desk. Overwhelmingly, the most popular way people interacted with a librarian was to visit the Ask a Librarian desk.

Who's Asking?

Librarians answer about 19-20 questions on Saturday and Sunday, as compared to an average of 45-50 questions per weekday. 45% of these questions were with UChicago students.

What do they want to know?

General Assistance & Directions

Fourty-three percent of tracked questions were tagged as 'general assistance,' in which librarians give users directions (11%), provide office supplies (7%) or refer the user to another service point such as circulation or the id & privileges office (18%).

Locating Resources

Twenty-six percent of transactions were tagged as 'known item', meaning that the patron has found something they are interesting in reading, but aren't sure how to access it in the library. This can include finding articles in databases (3%), locating items on reserve (3%), or finding a book in the stacks (11%).

Research Assistance

Twelve percent of questions logged were tagged as 'research assistance', meaning that the patron stopped by the reference desk asking for instruction in finding, using, or citing information.
